Buran – The Soviet Shuttle

In 1988 the USSR launched Buran, an unmanned space shuttle that completed two orbits and landed automatically — a feat no other shuttle has matched to this day.

Mir Space Station

Mir orbited Earth for 15 years (1986–2001) and was the first modular space station. Cosmonauts set endurance records aboard it, including Valeri Polyakov's 437 consecutive days in space.

Venera – Landing on Venus

Between 1970 and 1985 the Soviet Venera probes were the first spacecraft to land on another planet. Venera 13 survived Venus's crushing 90-atmosphere pressure long enough to send back colour photos.

N1 – The Moon Rocket

The Soviet N1 was the largest rocket ever attempted at the time — 105 m tall with 30 engines in its first stage. All four test flights between 1969–1972 ended in failure, and the programme was secretly cancelled.

First Spacewalk

On 18 March 1965, cosmonaut Alexei Leonov floated outside Voskhod 2 for 12 minutes — the first-ever spacewalk. His suit inflated so much he had to bleed air to squeeze back through the hatch.

First Woman in Space

Valentina Tereshkova orbited Earth 48 times aboard Vostok 6 on 16 June 1963 — becoming the first woman in space, a record the US wouldn't match for another 20 years.

Vostok 1 – First Human in Space

On 12 April 1961 Yuri Gagarin completed one orbit of Earth aboard Vostok 1, travelling at 27,400 km/h. The entire flight lasted just 108 minutes and changed history forever.

Laika – First Animal in Orbit

On 3 November 1957, a stray dog named Laika was launched aboard Sputnik 2, becoming the first living creature to orbit Earth. Her flight proved that a living organism could survive launch and weightlessness.

Sputnik 1 – The Beep Heard Around the World

On 4 October 1957, the USSR launched Sputnik 1, the first artificial satellite. Its 83.6 kg polished sphere orbited Earth every 96 minutes, and its radio "beep-beep" signal stunned the world.
